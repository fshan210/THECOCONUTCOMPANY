# Google Ranking Roadmap

Production: `https://cothecoconutcompany.com`

This roadmap prioritizes durable organic growth for a Kerala-rooted coconut food and beverage brand. It avoids fabricated claims, reviews, pricing, locations, credentials, and schema.

## Immediate: 0–14 days

### Technical and indexing

- Monitor Search Console until Page indexing finishes processing; classify every exclusion as intended, temporary, or actionable.
- Confirm the current 13-URL sitemap replaces older discovered URL counts and contains only 200/self-canonical pages.
- Authenticate Bing Webmaster Tools, import or verify the property, submit `/sitemap.xml`, and inspect the homepage.
- Decide on a 404 metadata approach that removes the inherited homepage canonical without redesigning the page.
- Establish weekly checks for status codes, canonicals, sitemap drift, robots changes, structured-data errors, and accidental noindex directives.
- Record baseline Core Web Vitals, organic impressions, branded/non-branded queries, indexed pages, referring domains, and conversions.

### Rich Results

- Keep Product data honest while products are preview-stage. Do not publish fake Offer, rating, or review fields merely to clear validation.
- Add complete Product offers when public price, currency, availability, and landing/purchase URLs are approved.
- Turn the highest-value three recipes into complete editorial specifications: instructions, author, cuisine, keywords, nutrition source, and optional video plan.

### Internal linking

- Map one primary search intent to every indexable URL.
- Add contextual links from homepage story sections to the strongest product, recipe, origin, and sustainability destinations.
- Link every recipe to the exact relevant product and every product to two or three relevant recipes.
- Preserve descriptive anchor text; avoid repeated generic “learn more” links where a topic-specific anchor is natural.

## 30-day plan

### Content clusters

Build four tightly connected clusters, each with one pillar page and supporting articles:

1. **Tender coconut water:** taste, serving cold, storage, recipe uses, Kerala origin, and product updates.
2. **Cooking with coconut:** coconut milk, oil, curries, breakfast, desserts, and practical ingredient guides.
3. **Kerala coconut culture:** farms, household rituals, regional food context, sourcing, and processing stories.
4. **Everyday coconut living:** refreshment rituals, seasonal recipes, entertaining, care, and gifting.

Each supporting page should link to its pillar, one adjacent article, one recipe, and one commercially relevant product page.

### Journal strategy

- Launch standalone `/journal/[slug]` pages instead of collection-only cards.
- Publish 4–6 substantial articles based on first-hand brand knowledge, approved sourcing facts, original photography, and named authors.
- Include publication and modification dates, author bios, Article/BlogPosting schema, descriptive image alt, and editorial references where claims require support.
- Start with topics that connect brand authority and search intent: Kerala coconut rituals, how coconut water is served, thoughtful processing, and cooking with coconut.

### Recipe strategy

- Launch standalone `/recipes/[slug]` pages for at least the top six recipes.
- Add visible numbered instructions, yield, prep/cook/total time, author, cuisine, keywords, tested ingredient quantities, and verified nutrition only when available.
- Add original step or final-dish imagery and optional short-form video.
- Create browse pages by meal and format only when each category has enough unique content to avoid thin archives.

### Entity SEO

- Keep Organization name, URL, logo, address scope, and sameAs profiles consistent across the site and official profiles.
- Complete Google Business Profile only if the business has an eligible, customer-facing or service-area presence.
- Use consistent `.CO | The Coconut Company`, Palakkad, Kerala, and coconut food/beverage descriptions across trusted profiles without keyword stuffing.
- Add approved founders and organization relationships to article author pages where factual and useful.

## 90-day plan

### Topical authority

- Publish two high-quality journal or recipe pages per month in each priority cluster, favoring depth and first-hand evidence over volume.
- Build hub pages that answer broad intent and distribute authority to supporting content.
- Refresh early articles with Search Console query data, clearer answers, stronger internal links, and updated visuals.
- Consolidate overlapping posts rather than creating multiple pages for the same intent.

### Backlinks and brand mentions

- Build a prospect list of Kerala food publications, beverage media, culinary schools, hospitality partners, sustainable packaging communities, and founder/business publications.
- Earn links through original assets: Kerala coconut guides, tested recipe collections, sourcing explainers, photography, and small data-led reports.
- Request links only where editorially relevant; avoid paid link schemes, bulk directories, reciprocal networks, and exact-match anchor campaigns.
- Reclaim unlinked `.CO` brand mentions and correct inconsistent domain or brand references.
- Create partner pages or case studies only for real collaborations with approval from all named parties.

### Digital PR

- Pitch seasonal stories around summer hydration rituals, Onam/holiday coconut cooking, regional ingredient education, and responsible processing.
- Package each pitch with an expert quote, original image set, concise fact sheet, and one canonical resource URL.
- Use founder commentary for product craft, Kerala context, and consumer rituals—not medical, investment, or unsupported sustainability claims.
- Track coverage quality, referral traffic, branded search lift, and links retained after 30/90 days.

### Search experience

- Use Search Console query/page reports to identify pages ranking positions 4–20 and improve them first.
- Strengthen titles and introductions around proven queries while preserving brand voice.
- Add concise answer blocks, comparison tables, or step lists only where they improve comprehension and can be supported factually.

## Six-month plan

### Authority system

- Maintain a content calendar balanced across product, recipe, origin, processing, sustainability, and lifestyle expertise.
- Build author/entity pages for recurring contributors with biographies and links to their work.
- Publish one flagship original resource each quarter, such as a Kerala coconut field guide or tested seasonal recipe report.
- Create a documented editorial QA process for accuracy, sourcing, image rights, schema parity, internal links, and update dates.

### Backlink portfolio

- Target a diverse profile of editorial links, recipe citations, supplier/partner mentions, local ecosystem references, and relevant industry resources.
- Review referring-domain growth quarterly; prioritize topical relevance, editorial placement, and organic referral traffic over raw link counts.
- Disavow links only when there is clear evidence of manipulative activity and a demonstrated need.

### Brand demand

- Coordinate PR, social, email, retail, and events so campaigns generate consistent branded searches and attributable mentions.
- Monitor variations of `.CO`, `The Coconut Company`, product names, founder names, and Kerala/coconut associations.
- Turn credible press, awards, certifications, retailers, and partnerships into visible on-site proof only after verification and permission.

### Measurement and iteration

- Review organic landing-page conversions, assisted conversions, engagement, index coverage, rich-result impressions, branded search, and share of non-branded traffic monthly.
- Compare content cohorts at 30, 90, and 180 days; update winners and consolidate or retire weak overlapping pages.
- Revalidate Product, Recipe, Article, Organization, and Breadcrumb markup after every content-model change.
- Reassess rendering and TTFB using field Core Web Vitals before considering architectural performance changes.

## Operating principles

- Publish fewer pages with genuine first-hand value rather than many thin pages.
- Keep visible content and structured data in exact parity.
- Never invent reviews, ratings, prices, credentials, addresses, nutrition, sustainability metrics, or health claims.
- Treat internal links as editorial guidance, not a keyword-placement exercise.
- Measure rankings by qualified non-branded visibility and conversions, not isolated keyword positions.
- Preserve the approved premium FMCG design system while improving discoverability.
